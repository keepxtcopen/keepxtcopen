So, there are two layers on this issue: GPL violations and mistreating 
users (tyranny). For the former, since the requests for source code were 
ignored as you said, it's best to contact the FSF licensing team after 
completing the checklist[1]. For the latter, we can help you with that 
by adding an entry to our malware pages under the tyranny category. But 
before we can do that, we need more information on the company, the 
device, and the software. You are also encouraged to write a paragraph 
(we call it a blurb) and send it to us. You can see [2] for an example.

a. the company. I have heard of BBK Electronics but I have never heard 
of Xiaotiancai (although I have moved out of China years ago). I 
remember BBK is/was popular because pretty much everyone knows of its 
advertising slogan, aired on TV years ago, about a text-to-speech device 
that reads out loud the text on English textbooks and helps learning 
English as a foreign language. Is Xiaotiancai popular? What do its 
product focus on? We may need to decide if we want to file this under 
BBK Electronics or Xiaotiancai. Is this[3] the correct company?

b. the device. What is the model of the device? Does it have a product 
page? What kind of purpose does the "smart" watch have? Is it for phone 
calling, health monitoring, or education purposes? Is it for parents or 
schools to spy on the children? We could say that the device rejects any 
other operating systems and disables some of the functionality as a 
punishment, but it's best that we cite a secondary source that is 
credible, like an online article or newspaper. By the way, how do you 
know the firmware is based on Linux and Android?

c. the software. Do you know how the company restricts user's right to 
install another operating system? Does the device have a locked 
bootloader? Does the system require talking to Xiaotiancai's server?

d. just for your own personal good: can you stop using this device? Are 
your parents or school forcing you to use it? If possible, I'd throw it 
away, send it for recycling.
